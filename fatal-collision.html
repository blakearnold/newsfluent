Published 1998

Fatal Collision Leads to Killer

close

A once ordinarily grim traffic collision has miraculously led to the identity of the Taxidermy Killer. G.W. Stubbs, a local of Frankfort, Michigan, has been linked to the dozens of murders attributed to the infamous serial killer, after being involved in a highway crash that led to the death of two others.

Once being found unconscious in his vehicle after the crash, authorities were quick to notice the foul stench emanating from his car’s trunk, which was broken open as a result of the accident. Upon further investigation, detectives pried open the trunk and discovered the body of Catherine Louise Blythe, an elementary school teacher from Grand Rapids who was reported missing four days prior.

Stubbs had been driving his vehicle, with his wife, Mary Beth Foley, and young daughter as passengers. An oncoming car passed over three lanes of traffic before colliding with Stubbs’ car, throwing all involved, except Stubbs himself, from their vehicles and out into highway traffic.

G.W. Stubbs and his daughter are still in critical condition. Mary Beth Foley was pronounced dead at the scene.
